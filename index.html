About

I am an inquisitive, hard working, determined engineering student who is interested in the intersection of mechanical design and control systems.

Work Experience

Synaptive Medical

At Synaptive I was on a team desigining a new version of their Drive product, an advanced device for neurosurgery. My role focused on the industrial electronics within the device where I drafted wiring diagrams to show how all parts were connected and selected connectors and designed cables to distribute power and provide communication lines for the many different components within the device.

Voxel8

In Voxel8's exciting startup phase I had the opportunity of working with their prototype units to design and develop 3D printed electronics and debug issues with the machines as they arose. I took on two especially interesting projects over my co-op term, one where I programmed an ATtiny84 microcontroller and built circuitry for it to function as a 3D printed watch and secondly where I developed a test fixture to measure the repeatability of the nozzle actuation mechanism to fractions of milimeters over tens of thousands of cycles and automatically store and plot the data.

Amphenol Canada

I focused primarily on mechanical design and drafting while at Amphenol, designing new connectors and updating and creating production and sales drawings in SolidWorks and AutoCAD. I also had the opportunity to design a fixture for automating the lifecycle testing of a new latching USB connector.

    In my 1A term at Waterloo, my group's culminating project was this handwriting robot which would store the user's unique strokes for every letter and then automatically print out typed messages on paper as if they were handwritten.

    I partipated on Team 610 for three years, including two as Strategy Lead. I designed and built robot subsystems, analyzed gameplay, and in 2013 was fortunate enough to be a part of the team that won the world championships.

    "Unstuckable" Toy

    In Grade 12 Technological Design, our main project was to build or modify toys so they could be used by children with disabilities. A partner and I designed and built a remote control car that would automatically turn away when it ran into an obstacle.
